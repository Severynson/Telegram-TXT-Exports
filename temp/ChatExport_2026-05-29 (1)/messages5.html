Hey @channel, we have three new QA warnings for passes off the rush, shot pressures behind the play, and missing shot blocks for the opposing team. Please review the information related to these warnings.


Passes Off the Rush:

The intent of this warning is to ensure that we properly annotate passes off the rush. This warning triggers when there is a non-off-the-rush pass within two seconds of an offensive zone entry. Annotators must check the events related to this warning and ensure that the pass type was properly annotated. You can find information related to passes off the rush here: https://training.sportlogiq.com/courses/hockey-course-4-the-offensive-zone/lessons/m10-offensive-zone-passes/.

Missing Block for the Opposing Team:

The intent of this warning is to ensure that we properly annotate shot blocks when a shot is marked as blocked. This warning populates when a blocked shot event does not have a corresponding blocked shot. Blocked shot events do not require a successful blocked shot. Each warning of this type requires verification. You can find information related to blocked shots here: https://training.sportlogiq.com/courses/hockey-course-5-defensive-events-miscellaneous/lessons/m14-block-opposition-shots/.

Shot Pressure Behind the Play:

The intent of this warning is to ensure that we properly annotate shot pressures for which there was a deflection. This warning triggers when there is a shot pressure farther than ten feet away from the shooter and on the non-goaltender side of the shooter. Annotators must review each event associated with these warnings and ensure that the events are in accordance with the conditions specified here: https://training.sportlogiq.com/courses/hockey-course-5-defensive-events-miscellaneous/lessons/m13-shot-pressure/.
