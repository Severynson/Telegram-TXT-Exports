Hi Alex,
Thank you for reaching out. That’s a great question and a very important topic in the sports analytics space right now.

From my experience, AI and computer vision are best used for large-scale data collection, tracking, tagging, and automating repetitive analysis tasks. They are incredibly effective for speeding up workflows and processing large amounts of information efficiently.

Where human involvement still matters most is in the interpretation of the game. Football is highly contextual, and understanding tactical intentions, player behaviors, decision-making, and the nuances behind the data still requires experienced analysts and coaches. Human oversight is also key for maintaining data quality and validating automated outputs.

I see the best balance as using AI to handle collection and first-layer analysis while analysts focus on interpretation, communication, and decision-making.
